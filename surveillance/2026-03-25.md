#### [2603.05706] *Reasoning Models Struggle to Control their Chains of Thought* — March 5, 2026
**Authors:** Chen Yueh-Han, Robert McCarthy, Bruce W. Lee, He He, Ian Kivlichan, **Bowen Baker**, Micah Carroll, Tomek Korbak
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05706

**Summary:** Introduces **CoT-Control evaluation suite** to measure whether reasoning models can control what they verbalize in chain-of-thought traces (e.g., solving a genetics problem without using the word "chromosome"). Finds reasoning models have significantly lower CoT controllability than output controllability (Claude Sonnet 4.5: 2.7% CoT control vs. 61.9% output control). CoT controllability decreases with more RL training, test-time compute, and problem difficulty.

**Track relevance:**
- (A) Pre-NeurIPS: Not directly relevant.
- (B) Semester paper: Not relevant.
- (C) MCTS: COMPLEMENTARY — key insight that more RL training and test-time compute reduce CoT controllability. Important safety consideration if building MCTS-style reasoning systems; the verbalized search trace may be harder to steer than the output.
